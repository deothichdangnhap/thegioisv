The Life of Tigers

Discover the World of Tigers

Learn about these powerful and elusive big cats, their hunting skills, and their critical role in the ecosystem.

About Tigers

Tigers are solitary and powerful big cats known for their strength and agility. They communicate using vocalizations, scent markings, and body language.

Habitat & Distribution

Tigers inhabit forests, mangroves, and grasslands across Asia, including India, Russia, and Southeast Asia. They require large territories to thrive.

Diet & Hunting

Tigers are apex predators and primarily hunt deer, wild boar, and other large herbivores. They rely on stealth, strength, and powerful bites to take down prey.

Social Structure

Unlike lions, tigers are solitary animals. Males and females only come together for mating, and mothers raise cubs alone until they are independent.

Conservation Efforts

Tiger populations are threatened by habitat loss and poaching. Conservationists work to protect them through anti-poaching laws, habitat restoration, and breeding programs.
